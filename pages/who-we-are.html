Meet the Team

Researchers

Prof. Steven Desch

Principal Investigator

Steve Desch, a theoretical astrophysicist, combines meteoritic data with astrophysical modeling to study solar system history and planet formation. His interests include chondrule formation, radionuclide origins, and protoplanetary disk processes. He leads Task 2, modeling element redistribution in protoplanetary disks, and contributes to Task 1, modeling chemical fractionations during molecular cloud core collapse.

Dr. Kara Brugman

Co-Investigator

Task 4 Lead

Kara Brugman is an experimental petrologist studying magma genesis, volatile solubility, and magmatic system timescales on Earth, exoplanets, and solar system bodies. She leads the Task 4 team, conducting experiments on melting curves and bioessential element solubilities for exoplanet compositions, and modeling changes in upper mantle and crust compositions as heat fluxes decline.

Prof. Hilairy Hartnett

Co-Investigator

Dr. Alan Jackson

Co-Investigator

Tasks 2 & 3

Dr. Jackson, an astronomer and planetary scientist, studies planetary system formation and evolution, focusing on the final stages of terrestrial planet assembly. For Task 3, he conducts SPH simulations of giant impacts to quantify changes in mantle and bulk-planet elemental abundances. He also contributes to Task 2, examining how orbital eccentricity affects pebble accretion rates.

Prof. Larry Nittler

Co-Investigator

Tasks 2b & 3d

Larry is a cosmochemist and planetary scientist studying star and solar system origins through meteorite analysis and remote sensing. He has led roles in NASA’s Stardust, Genesis, and MESSENGER missions and works on Hayabusa2 and BepiColombo. He won the Alfred O. Nier prize, became a Meteoritical Society fellow, and has Asteroid 5992 Nittler named after him.

Prof. Molly Simon

Co-Investigator

Task 5

Dr. Molly Simon’s research focuses on using citizen science to provide data-rich experiences for undergraduate students both in-person and online. She also assesses the effectiveness of new active learning strategies designed to enhance student retention in college-level astronomy and planetary science courses.

Prof. Christy Till

Co-Investigator

Task 4

Dr. Christy Till is a geologist and professor specializing in magma formation and planetary geology. Her work on volcanoes, subduction zones, and exoplanet magma has earned her several awards, including the 2024 Daly Lecture. She is also dedicated to advancing diversity and inclusion in science, having held leadership roles in related initiatives at ASU and the SZ4D program.
